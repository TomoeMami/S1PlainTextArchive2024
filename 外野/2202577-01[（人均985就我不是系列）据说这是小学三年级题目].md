To determine the volume of the remaining cement in the cubic block after accounting for the cylindrical holes, we'll follow a systematic approach using principles from geometry and the inclusion-exclusion principle.

### **1. Calculate the Volume of the Cube**

The cube has an edge length of 20 cm.

\[

\text{Volume of the cube} = \text{edge length}^3 = 20^3 = 8000 \, \text{cm}^3

\]

### **2. Calculate the Volume of the Cylindrical Holes**

Each face of the cube has a cylindrical hole with a diameter of 10 cm, so the radius \( r \) is 5 cm. There are three pairs of opposite faces, resulting in **three perpendicular cylinders** passing through the cube.

The volume \( V_{\text{cyl}} \) of one cylinder is:

\[

V_{\text{cyl}} = \pi r^2 h = 3.14 \times 5^2 \times 20 = 3.14 \times 25 \times 20 = 1570 \, \text{cm}^3

\]

Since there are three cylinders:

\[

\text{Total volume of cylinders} = 3 \times 1570 = 4710 \, \text{cm}^3

\]

### **3. Account for Overlapping Volumes**

When multiple cylinders intersect, simply subtracting their volumes would lead to double-counting the overlapping regions. To correct this, we use the **inclusion-exclusion principle**.

- **Pairwise Intersections (\( V_{12}, V_{13}, V_{23} \))**:

  

  The volume where any two cylinders intersect (known as the Steinmetz solid) is:

  \[

  V_{\text{pairwise}} = \frac{16r^3}{3} = \frac{16 \times 5^3}{3} = \frac{2000}{3} \approx 666.67 \, \text{cm}^3

  \]

  

  For three pairs:

  \[

  \text{Total pairwise intersections} = 3 \times 666.67 \approx 2000 \, \text{cm}^3

  \]

- **Triple Intersection (\( V_{123} \))**:

  

  The volume where all three cylinders intersect is:

  \[

  V_{\text{triple}} = 8r^3 = 8 \times 5^3 = 1000 \, \text{cm}^3

  \]

### **4. Apply the Inclusion-Exclusion Principle**

\[

V_{\text{union}} = V_{\text{cylinders}} - V_{\text{pairwise intersections}} + V_{\text{triple intersection}} = 4710 - 2000 + 1000 = 3710 \, \text{cm}^3

\]

### **5. Determine the Remaining Cement Volume**

\[

\text{Remaining cement volume} = V_{\text{cube}} - V_{\text{union}} = 8000 - 3710 = 4290 \, \text{cm}^3

\]

### **Final Answer**

The volume of the remaining cement after accounting for the cylindrical holes is **4290 cm³**.
